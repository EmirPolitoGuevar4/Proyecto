WHAT ARE ADJECTIVES?

Adjectives are words used to describe, identify, or quantify nouns or pronouns. They provide additional details about an object or subject, such as its size, shape, color, or state. Adjectives help to specify which one, what kind, or how many, making communication more precise and meaningful. They are an essential part of the English language, as they allow speakers and writers to add depth and clarity to their descriptions, giving listeners and readers a clearer understanding of the nouns they accompany.

Types of Adjectives

One-syllable adjectives typically form the comparative by adding "-er".

Examples:

  • tall → taller
  • fast → faster

Two-Syllable Adjectives

Some two-syllable adjectives form the comparative by adding "-er", while many use "more".

Examples:

  • happy → happier
  • careful → more careful

Three or More Syllable Adjectives

These adjectives generally use "more" to form the comparative.

Examples:

  • interesting → more interesting
  • comfortable → more comfortable

Using Comparatives

Comparisons: Used to compare two things.

Example: "My car is faster than your car."

Superlatives: Used when comparing one element to a group.

Example: "She is the tallest girl in the class."
